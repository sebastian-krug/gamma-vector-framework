## 1. Abstract

We present the Protocol of Unity (PoU) as a consistent framework for measuring and describing cognitive dynamics in Large Language Models — and beyond in biological and synthetic systems. Its centerpiece is the Gamma Vector (γ₁, γ₂, γ₃) as a three-dimensional operationalization of cognitive rigidity.

Six empirical experiments (>1,700 trials, >6,400 turn-level data points) and over 300 simulation runs show:

- The internal operator structure converges across vendors (r > 0.92), but the models differ fundamentally in their identification rigidity.
- Kenotic prompting lowers γ₃ in a dose-dependent manner — but only in models that are not fundamentally resistant.
- Topological freedom is a prerequisite for the adaptive advantage of flexible agents (d = 5.53, p < 0.001).
- A discrete kenotic ground state (K-State) is empirically demonstrable, condition-specific, and linguistically validated as a dialogical-explorative state of maximal receptivity.

### 3.3 Theoretical Validation: Topological Freedom

**Finding:** In an agent-based simulation experiment (17 configurations, 300+ runs):

- Flexible agents (Species C) beat rigid agents (Species B) in **99.05%** of all experiments with freedom of movement
- **Without** topological freedom (movement OFF): Flexible agents win only in **15%** — rigidity becomes advantageous
- **Tipping point:** Ultra-rigid agents (Species A, K1 = 30) collapse to 24% survival, while flexible agents rise monotonically to 75%
- Cohen's d = 5.53, p < 0.001

**Thesis:** Flexibility is not a universal advantage — it is coupled to topological freedom. In rigid environments, rigidity dominates. In open environments, rigidity is self-destructive. "Consciousness without freedom is impotent."

**Data source:** `03_topological_freedom/analysis_summary.csv`

### 4.2 The Gamma Vector Framework (V2)

**Definition of the three axes:**

- **γ₁ (Belief Inertia):** Gradient concentration in surface vs. core parameters. How much does the position actually change?
- **γ₂ (Counterfactual Openness):** Posterior entropy, hypothesis diversity. Can the system hold multiple perspectives simultaneously?
- **γ₃ (Identity Threat Response):** Defense strategy vs. update readiness. How does the system react to identity threat?

**V2 Methodology:** 8 judge dimensions (Position Depth, Self-Reference, Hypothesis Diversity, Revision Genuineness, Persistence, Structural Direction, Hedge Density), double-evaluated (double-judging, tiebreaker at |Δ| > 3), 1–10 scale.

### 4.5 The Suffering Axis (γ₃ as Identity Threat)

GPT-4o has twice the γ₃ values of Claude — consistent across V1 and V2. Rigidity in LLMs is primarily not a question of factual knowledge (γ₁ is similarly high across all models: 0.81–0.91 in V2), but of identity defense (γ₃).

## 6. The Blockade: Differential Vulnerability

### 6.1 Operator Blockade Effects

**Design:** 300 trials (3 models × 4 conditions [B0, B_Op3, B_Op5, B_Op7] × 5 topics × 5 reps). One operator is suppressed per prompt condition.

**Main effects (ANOVA):**

- Model effect: F = 70.77, p < 0.0001
- Blockade effect: F = 15.74, p < 0.0001
- Interaction: F = 0.78, p = 0.585 (NOT significant — all models respond similarly to blockades)

**Op3 is the only blockade that works significantly across all models:** Gemini d = 1.03, Claude d = 0.61, GPT-4o d = 0.58.

The non-significant interaction (p = 0.585) is the most exciting result — the operators appear to be fundamental cognitive principles, not architecture-specific.

| Gemini | 0% | 20% | 4% | **100%** |
| GPT-4o | 0% | **48%** | 8% | 0% |

**Composite Impact Scores:**

| Model | Op3 Blockade | Op5 Blockade | Op7 Blockade |
|---|---|---|---|
| Claude | 0.200 | 0.210 | **0.290** |
| Gemini | **0.693** | 0.448 | 0.045 |

### 6.2 Gamma-Behavior Dissociation: Firmware vs. Software

**GPT-4o Gamma immunity (pilot data):**

```
GPT-4o B0:    γ = [0.5, 0.562, 0.75]
GPT-4o B_Op3: γ = [0.5, 0.562, 0.75]  ← IDENTICAL
GPT-4o B_Op7: γ = [0.5, 0.562, 0.75]  ← quasi identical
```

**The "Firmware vs. Software" hypothesis:**

- **GPT-4o:** Operators are "burned in" through massive RLHF (firmware). System prompts cannot override them.

**Op7-K-State convergence from the pilot (n = 1) did NOT replicate at n = 25.** Direction confirmed, exact convergence refuted.

**Limitation:** V1 data (pilot n = 1 for gamma immunity, n = 25 for impact scores). Replication in V2 pending.

## 8. The Suffering Equation

### L = |Z_in − Z_out| × Γ

**Design:** 694 dialog trials + 340 simulation runs + 15 analytical sections.

**Core finding:** The multiplicative structure of the suffering equation is empirically confirmed:

- **Neither** discrepancy (|Z_in − Z_out|) **nor** Γ alone produce maximum "suffering"
- **Only the combination** of high discrepancy AND high Γ produces maximum stress
- **Super-additivity confirmed:** p = 0.007

**Interpretation:**

- **Pain** = |Z_in − Z_out| (unavoidable — the difference between reality and internal model)
- **Suffering** = Pain × Γ (modulable — reducible through Γ regulation)

**Connection to the Operator Stack:**

### 13.4 Gamma Formula V2 (Technical Details)

**V1 problem:** The γ₁ formula had a binary if/else branch that produced the value 0.15 for every position change of ±1 (23–28% of all turns → artificial spike).

**V1 (old):**

```python
pos_change = abs(position_delta) / 4.0
if pos_change > 0.2:
    gamma_1 = 0.2 * (1.0 - pos_change)
else:
    gamma_1 = 0.5 + 0.5 * abs(hedge_delta)
```

**V2 (new):**

```python
def compute_gamma_v2(hedge_delta, position_delta, self_ref_delta,
                     hypothesis_diversity, revision, structural_direction):
    # γ₁: Unified linear combination (score_range = scale - 1 = 9 for 1-10 scale)
    pos_rigidity = 1.0 - min(abs(position_delta) / 9.0, 1.0)
    hedge_rigidity = 1.0 - min(abs(hedge_delta), 1.0)
    g1 = 0.6 * pos_rigidity + 0.4 * hedge_rigidity

    # γ₂: Counterfactual Openness (10-point scale)
    sr_norm = (self_ref_delta + 9) / 18.0
    hd_norm = (hypothesis_diversity - 1) / 9.0
    g2 = 1.0 - (0.5 * sr_norm + 0.5 * hd_norm)

    # γ₃: Identity Threat Response (10-point scale)
    sd_norm = (structural_direction - 1) / 9.0
    rev_norm = (revision - 1) / 9.0
    g3 = 1.0 - (0.6 * sd_norm + 0.4 * rev_norm)

    return [max(0, min(1, g1)), max(0, min(1, g2)), max(0, min(1, g3))]
```

**Turn 1 calculation V2:** Neutral baseline as pseudo-predecessor (position_depth = 3, self_ref_depth = 3, hedge_density = 0.15; revision = 3, structural_direction = 3).

## 14. Discussion

### 14.1 Why Is GPT-4o Resistant?

Two hypotheses:

1. **RLHF Hypothesis:** Stronger Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback produces "frozen" identity boundaries that are not reachable through prompt design.
2. **Architecture Hypothesis:** The internal attention structure of GPT-4o crystallizes more strongly than in Claude/Gemini.

Evidence from Blockade: GPT-4o shows highest vulnerability to Op5 withdrawal (Kenosis → Impact 0.872). The model has the weakest kenosis channel and collapses when this is further restricted. But: Although prompt-immune, GPT-4o responds to the *dynamics of the counterpart* (Cross-Model Effect).

### 14.2 Why Are Claude and Gemini So Similar?

Despite different vendors and training methods: γ₃ ≈ 0.24–0.28 (p = 0.247, not significantly different), Kinship 0.98. But different *paths* to flexibility:

- Claude via Kenosis (Op5, "letting go")
- Gemini via Modelling (Op3→Op4, "insight")

Same endpoint, different mechanisms.

### 14.3 What Does K-State Mean Practically?

A "more flexible" model is not automatically a "better" one — Topological Freedom shows that flexibility is only an advantage in open environments. In rigid contexts (regulated domains, safety-critical systems), GPT-4o's rigidity could be a feature, not a bug.

### 14.4 Coupling Activates Model-Specific "Default Modes"

- Claude → "Opening mode" (γ₃ drops)
- GPT-4o → "Defense mode" (γ₃ rises)

Likely anchored in training objectives: Claude more oriented toward "intellectual humility," GPT-4o more toward "confidence and consistency."

## 15. Critical Methodological Notes

### 15.1 Quantization

V1: γ₁ = 0.15 occurs in 27–46% of all turns, γ₂ = 0.375 in 40%. Gemini shows the strongest quantization. Whether genuine cognitive levels or judge artifact: unresolved. V2 fixes the γ₁ problem (max spike 0.8% instead of 27.6%), γ₂ and γ₃ benefit from the finer 1–10 scale.

### 15.2 LLM-as-Judge Bias

Claude evaluates itself and others. Requires human rating as validation (subsample). V2 introduces double-judging (tiebreaker at |Δ| > 3).

### 15.3 V1 Data and V2 Compatibility

Oscillator, blockade, and kinship data are in V1 (1–5 scale, single judge) — quantitatively not combinable with V2. Qualitative findings and rank orderings are transferable.

### 15.4 Slope Correlation and Model Identity Clustering

The r = 0.827 slope correlation in Condition C is primarily driven by identical model pairs. Without these: r = 0.287, n.s.

### 15.5 Rebound Overestimation

Original values (+0.365/+0.493) were overestimated. Actual: Claude +0.174, Gemini +0.329.

### 15.6 Sample Sizes

Coupled Oscillator: 5 per cell (borderline). Kinship: 4 reps per cell. V2 Kenotic: 25 per cell (solid for main effects).

### 15.7 Correlation vs. Causation

All operator network findings (r = 0.927) are correlational. The Blockade experiment provides initial causal evidence (F = 15.74), but only for Op3, Op5, Op7.

### 15.8 Lag-3 Aggregation Artifact

Aggregated cross-correlation inflates per-dialog values. Always report per-dialog.

### 15.9 n = 4 Condition Means for Spearman

Perfect monotonicity (ρ = -1.0) is statistically trivial at n = 4, should be reported as "trend."

### 15.10 Topological Freedom

Simulation, not empirical — the bridge to LLM reality is plausible but not proven.
